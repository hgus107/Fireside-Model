About the Model

The Fireside Model is a polls-free forecasting framework for U.S. midterm House elections. It uses only publicly available economic data from federal statistical agencies, with no reliance on polls, approval ratings, or expert ratings.

The model measures economic conditions across a 21-month window from February of the president's inauguration year through October of the midterm election year. This captures the full economic trajectory as experienced by voters before Election Day.

Eleven economic variables were tested across two categories: Consumer Direct (pocketbook) indicators and Institutional (labor market) indicators. Systematic combination testing identified five variables that collectively explain 83 percent of the variation in House seat changes for the president's party.

Note: The 1994 Republican Revolution is an example of a major non-economic event that produced an outcome outside the model's predictive range.

Calculation Technique

The model uses Ordinary Least Squares (OLS) linear regression estimated on 12 midterm elections from 1978 through 2022.

All percentage changes are calculated as: ((Value at October of midterm year) minus (Value at February of inauguration year)) divided by (Value at February of inauguration year), multiplied by 100.

Consumer Sentiment is measured as the point change in the index value, not a percentage change.

Initial Jobless Claims uses the weekly value nearest to the February and October reference dates.

GDP Growth is the average annualized quarterly growth rate across all quarters in the data window.

All source data is freely available from the Federal Reserve Economic Data (FRED) database at fred.stlouisfed.org.
